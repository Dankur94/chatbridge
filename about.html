Our Story

Our Journey

It started at RWTH Aachen.

An engineer and a physicist — two students who shared an obsession with getting to the root of things. Never the surface. Always the why.

Daniel moved to Hong Kong — into the heart of the Greater Bay Area, surrounded by factories that build the machines the world runs on. He saw the same problem everywhere: brilliant hardware, built to spec — but when it came to selling in Europe, the documentation didn't pass.

Risk assessments that didn't follow ISO 12100. Technical files with gaps. CE marks affixed without the legal foundation. Machines held at customs. Sales lost.

The new EU Machinery Regulation (2023/1230) makes this even more urgent. From January 2027, the rules tighten. Manufacturers who don't adapt will lose access to the European market.

That's why we started KurthTechnik.

We bridge the gap between Chinese manufacturing excellence and EU regulatory requirements. A German engineer who sits in the factory — and reads the regulation.
