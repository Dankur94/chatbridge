The Greater Bay Area

86 million people. 11 cities. China's manufacturing heartland — and where we sit, between your factory floor and EU regulation.

Overview

What is the Greater Bay Area?

The Guangdong-Hong Kong-Macao Greater Bay Area (GBA) is a Chinese national strategy integrating 11 cities in the Pearl River Delta into one connected economic zone — comparable to the San Francisco Bay Area or Greater Tokyo, but larger.

For machinery manufacturers, the GBA is one of the world's most concentrated production regions. Cobots, CNC machines, packaging equipment, automation systems — they're designed and built here. And increasingly, they're exported to Europe.

For Machinery Manufacturers

Why the GBA matters
for EU compliance

China's Machinery Export Hub

The GBA is where a significant share of China's machinery exports originate. Cobots from Shenzhen, CNC machines from Guangzhou, packaging lines from Dongguan — all heading to Europe.

On-Site Access

Being in Hong Kong means we can visit any GBA factory within 2 hours. We review machines, check documentation, and discuss with your engineers — in person, not over email.

Hong Kong as Legal Gateway

Hong Kong operates under Common Law with strong IP protection and English as business language. An ideal base for bridging Chinese manufacturing and European regulatory requirements.

The 2027 Deadline

The new EU Machinery Regulation (2023/1230) applies from January 2027. GBA manufacturers exporting to Europe need to update their documentation now — or risk losing market access.

Direct Flights from Europe

Frankfurt → Hong Kong (11h), Frankfurt → Shenzhen (11h), Frankfurt → Guangzhou (11h). High-speed rail connects all GBA cities internally.
